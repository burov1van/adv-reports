Glossary

CTR (Click-Through Rate) – the percentage ratio of the number of clicks on an ad unit to its number of impressions. It is considered a conditional measure of an ad’s or banner’s attractiveness to the audience to which it is shown.
Time Targeting – displaying ads only on specified days of the week and times of day according to a schedule.
Time on Site – the time difference between the first and last event in a visit (events include page views, external link clicks, file downloads, goal completions, and other events). It is conventionally considered the time a user spent on the site.
Geographic Queries (Toponym Queries) – queries that contain a place name (e.g., “air conditioners in Moscow”).
Geo-Targeting (Geographic Targeting) – displaying ads in a specific geographic area.
Page Depth – the number of pages viewed in a single visit.
Campaign (Advertising Campaign) – an advertising activity planned for a specific period, geography, and budget within which ads are displayed.
Click – a user’s action of clicking on an ad. It is considered an indication of visiting the advertiser’s site or viewing the advertiser’s contact information.
Keywords – words carrying significant semantic meaning. They serve as triggers for showing ads when they appear in a user’s search query or on a site page.
Conversions – the number of visits during which users completed a target action (e.g., visited specified areas of the site, viewed a certain number of pages, submitted a form, or made a call).
Contextual Advertising – a type of online advertising that displays ads based on users’ search queries or the content/context of a web page.
Negative Keywords – words for which the ad will not be shown. They help narrow the target audience and improve the effectiveness of contextual advertising. Example: car -repair -service will show ads for “car sale,” “car rental,” and all queries containing that phrase except “car repair” and “car service.”
Ad (Contextual Ad) – advertising material containing promotional information and a link to the advertiser’s site or virtual business card.
Bounces – a visit is considered a bounce if no more than one page view was recorded during the visit; the visit duration is less than the defined bounce threshold (default 15 seconds); and no “non-bounce” event was recorded.
Display Link – the site address shown in the ad.
Behavioral Targeting – showing ads to users who have recently demonstrated certain interests or performed specific online actions (e.g., browsing travel services or financial news).
Search Query – the text query entered by a user in Yandex or Google search, or in the search form of a site within the Yandex or Google Advertising Network.
Impression – the display of an ad to a user.
Advertising Site – a website or mobile app that hosts advertisements.
Ad Networks – systems for placing ads on websites, mobile apps, and Smart TV apps. These platforms display ad units from Yandex or Google.
Relevance – the degree to which ad content matches the user’s query.
Remarketing (Retargeting) – a mechanism for reaching users who previously visited your site, viewed certain sections, or performed specific actions, such as adding a product to the cart without completing the purchase.
Site – a collection of pages connected by meaning and navigation, serving as a tool for promoting a product, service, or company image online.
URL (Link) – the address of a site or page on the internet. For example, the link in an ad leading to the advertiser’s site or a page containing the advertiser’s contact information.
Socio-Demographic Targeting – showing ads to user groups with certain demographic characteristics, such as gender, age, or social status (e.g., unmarried women aged 20–35 without children).
Targeting – a mechanism for selecting a subset of the audience that meets specified criteria (the target audience) and showing ads specifically to them (e.g., time targeting, geo-targeting, contextual advertising, behavioral targeting).
Thematic Targeting – displaying ads on websites that match a specific theme.
Target Audience – users who meet certain criteria and represent the highest value to the advertiser.
